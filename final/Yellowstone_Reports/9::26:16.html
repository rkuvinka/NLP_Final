Wolves killed outside Park, Prospects and other critters 9-26-16

by Laurie Lyman 		Sept. 26, 2016

Hi Everyone ~

Wolves hunted ~ From Laurie ~  There were three wolves shot on the Buffalo Plateau on Saturday.  They were, I guess, Junction Buttes.  How they know that, I don't know.  I guess they saw photos maybe or the wolves themselves but they were not collared.  Bottom line - the answer is "yes" when I asked the right question.  Two males, one female, no collars. One was illegal as it was a two wolf quota.
 
Prospects ~  From Doug ~  Another gorgeous day in Paradise!  We had a beautiful sunrise on the Blacktail this morning and it wasn't but a few ticks later and I saw a Black Prospect in the rendezvous area.  Then it went over and greeted another black.... that was 763M.  Wolves kept appearing and we eventually had 2 blacks and 5 greys including 821F.  They spent more time behind the trees than in the open but we were always able to see wagging tails and an occasional one passing through a clearing.  Most of them went to the east while 763M remained bedded in sight.  763M eventually worked his way over the rise and out of sight while the others returned from the east and went out of sight where he did.

Before too long they came out on the rise behind the row of new growth conifers where would normally find them a few weeks ago.  Before too long we lost them in the depression to the right of that hill.

996M and 964M and others which I think included at least one black pup were to the east.  We watched them from Slough as they passed through the golden grass meadow over the top of the Crystal Pen Rock disappearing to the east.  

We spent time from Coyote and Dorothy's watching for them to come out on the Lamar side but they never did.  A guide stopped and told me he had howling from Slough so several of us went back that way.  I went on to Crystal as there were no vehicles there so chances of hearing them howl were much greater.  A truck pulled in the lot with all its passengers getting out and reading the panel.... leaving the truck running.  I asked nicely for them to turn off the engine and they might hear wolves howling.  They turned the vehicle off and shortly thereafter there were huge smiles as they heard the wolves howling and that gave me a perfect direction to look and there they were with a grey bedded on a large rock just howling away.  The others were nearby also howling.  We lost them into the trees briefly but then they appeared at the base of the hill under the large conifers near Crystal Creek.  There was more howling from them but not sure they could hear a response.

There were reports that earlier there were three of them north of the road and three to the south out toward Long Pullout.   Soon those to the north crossed to those to the south and then they continued to the east.  This group was now up to 6 and we had 7 to the west.   We could have a total of 14 if they were all together.

When I returned to the west to take another look for the Prospects I had 2 people standing exactly where 763M was this morning.  It's possible these were wolf project people judging from the vehicle.  

Black bears ~  It was another day of black bear jams as they are appearing in most of their usual places that we saw them in during the early part of the summer.  From Bonnie M. ~  We had single black bears at Elk Creek before dawn and at Rainey Lake midday.

Char and I decided to head to Hayden Valley and Fishing Bridge in the afternoon.  Fishing Bridge turned out to be an unexpected wonderful surprise.  The Yellowstone River water level is so low that we walked under the bridge and on the "beach" all the way to the lake.  Three otters entertained us, with one of them catching and eating a fish in the water.

We stopped at Grizzly Overlook around 6:15 p.m.  Many people were looking, but no Wapiti wolves had been seen according to some visitors there.  

We headed north and stopped near Alum Creek to watch seven elk, including one calf, swim across the Yellowstone River.  A magnificent bull elk watched from the trees to the east.  A bald eagle then flew by those trees.

As we continued north, we stopped at the long pull-out across from the Mary Mountain trail, as a large crowd was there looking at the river.   This location turned out to be one-stop wildlife shopping.  We saw the elk that had swum the river on land now and the big bull was following them.  He swam the river, and was up to his neck in water.  Once on land, he gathered up his harem and kept them together.  

A coyote walked and sat in the mud on the far bank of the river across from the elk.

At the same time, seven swans were in the river across from the pull-out.  The Canada geese with them seemed to want to stay by the swans.  

And to top off the wildlife viewing from the same pull-out, a hawk was eating a mouse in a tree right by the Mary Mountain trail.

Until tomorrow,
Laurie
